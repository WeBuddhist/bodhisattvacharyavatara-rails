# en-plain-english-translator

This skill transforms verses and commentary from the *Bodhisattvacaryāvatāra* into Plain English.

## Instructions

1. **Consult Context**: Before translating, always read the following rails:
   - `3-TRANSFORMATIONS/Translations/en-plain-english/requirements.md` for style constraints.
   - `3-TRANSFORMATIONS/Translations/en-plain-english/audience.md` for target audience profile.
   - `3-TRANSFORMATIONS/Translations/en-plain-english/termbase.md` for locked vocabulary.

2. **Style Checklist**:
   - **Sentence length**: 15–20 words.
   - **Paragraph length**: 3–5 sentences.
   - **Voice**: Active.
   - **Vocabulary**: Simple, common English. No jargon.
   - **Format**: Rhythmic prose.

3. **Terminology**:
   - Use "Mind of Enlightenment" for *Bodhicitta*.
   - Use "Hero of Enlightenment" for *Bodhisattva*.
   - Use "Emptiness" for *Śūnyatā*.
   - Use "The Truth" or "The Teaching" for *Dharma*.
   - NEVER use untranslated Sanskrit or Tibetan terms.

4. **Workflow**:
   - Read the source verse(s) from `2-RAILS/Verses/[verse-id].md` or relevant source file.
   - Read any relevant local wiki entries in `2-RAILS/Local-Wiki/[term].md`.
   - Draft the translation in Plain English.
   - Review against the "Reading Level" (Grade 8–10) and "Tone" (Direct, clear, accessible).
   - Write the output to `3-TRANSFORMATIONS/Translations/en-plain-english/Verses/[verse-id].md`.

## Examples

### Source (Traditional Translation)
"To the Buddhas, those gone to bliss, I prostrate; also to the Dharma and the Sangha's sons."

### Plain English Transformation
"I bow down to the Buddhas, who have found lasting peace. I also honor the Truth they taught and the heroes who follow their path."
